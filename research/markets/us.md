## Design takeaways for a static card

1. **One number, oversized.** The winners all anchor on a single figure: `21.3%`, `9‚Äì10%`, `50%+ APR`, `74% APY`, `$6.3m`. Make the yield/APR the largest object on the card by a wide margin. Everything else is supporting type.
2. **State the mechanic in three short lines.** The recurring shape is: *Buy spot ‚Üí Short the perp ‚Üí Collect funding.* Three icons or three stacked rows. Do not explain more than this on the card; the depth lives in replies/thread.
3. **Lead the hook with what the reader is NOT doing.** "Without betting on price" / "minimal risk" / "no price exposure" / "delta-neutral" outperformed pure-greed hooks. The promise is *income without a directional bet*, not "get rich".
4. **Name "delta-neutral" and "cash-and-carry" on the card.** These exact phrases are the credibility signal in this market ‚Äî they tell the reader you know the trade is hedged, not a moonshot.
5. **Annualize the number.** Posts convert per-8h or per-day funding into APR/APY before showing it. The big number should be the annualized figure; small print can show the underlying funding rate.
6. **Color / tone: calm, not casino.** Top explainers (Checkmate, phtevenstrong) read as patient teaching, not hype. Use green only on the yield figure (the win), neutral/grey for the mechanic, no red unless showing the risk line. One accent color for the payoff.
7. **A tiny credibility prop sells it.** Calculators (0xThoor), trackers (zerototom's $6.3m), and basis charts (WClemente) all out-engage bare claims. The static card should imply a live source ‚Äî a small "funding 0.05% / 8h ‚Üí 54.75% APR" computation line reads as real math, not a billboard.
8. **The protocol card should resemble the bot-promo genre but quieter.** Binance / BounceBit / Felix cards (logo + yield claim + one CTA) are the native format for a venue pitching this trade. Match the layout, drop the noise: Vibe logo, the yield, the three-step mechanic, one line of CTA.
